# ETHICS

GENARCH is designed for educational scientific literacy and explicitly avoids individual medical inference.

## Non-negotiable constraints

1. No accounts, login, or persistent user identity.
2. No personal genomic uploads (VCF/FASTQ/consumer kit raw files).
3. No individual risk scores or “high-risk” labels.
4. No diagnosis or treatment recommendations.
5. Passport generation is stateless and non-identifying.

## Why no individual risk scoring

Individualized scoring from population-level summary evidence risks overinterpretation, misuse, and inequitable harm,
especially given uneven ancestry representation in many datasets. GENARCH instead presents evidence with explicit
limitations and confidence tiers.

## Equity commitments

- Disease pages include population limitation statements.
- Graph edges include ancestry representation metadata.
- Community module uses neutral language (“higher modeled burden”).
- Sources remain public and aggregate to avoid re-identification risk.

## Privacy stance

- No PII collection
- No user-linked analytics requirements
- No persistent passport form storage

## Communication standard

Every scientific page includes an Evidence & Limitations block and educational-only disclaimer.
